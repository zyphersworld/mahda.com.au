Curriculum Benchmark Analysis

How the Crossroads curriculum compares, and where the College sits.

This analysis sets out how the Crossroads curriculum is positioned relative to recognised training benchmarks. Crossroads is an independent, non-accredited college; its awards are internal and are recognised by many churches and mission organisations for ministry purposes.

The Advanced Diploma of Theology is an internal award the College regards as broadly comparable to AQF Level 6 in depth of study, while remaining distinct from nationally accredited qualifications.

Study-hour expectations

Foundation Studies: twenty hours or more per Study Unit (seven units). Module Course: forty hours or more per Module Unit across three Series.

Where Crossroads sits

Crossroads International Bible College is part of the Full Gospel International Church, an independent denomination affiliated with the worldwide World Evangelism Fellowship. It stands within historic classical Pentecostal faith and affirms the core doctrines commonly recognised within the Australian Christian Churches (A.C.C.).

While similar to numerous Bible colleges, Crossroads does not hold accreditation and does not pursue recognition from national accrediting agencies. Many churches, denominations, and mission organisations nonetheless accept its qualifications as valid credentials for ministry.
