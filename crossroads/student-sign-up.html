Courses overview

We offer four levels of study, each designed to deepen understanding of biblical principles and equip students for Christian living and service.

Level 1

Spiritual Growth Series

A hyperlinked booklet with many teaching booklets covering an extensive array of doctrines. The Series offers a six-session outline — which can be extended or tailored — for teaching new Christians the foundational truths of God's Word. People learn progressively: line upon line, precept upon precept (Isaiah 28:10).

View series →

Level 2

75+ Teaching Booklets

Approximately 75+ booklets covering biblical teaching, Christian living, and counselling, drawn directly from the Word of God. These booklets contain no questions; they teach, refresh, and edify all members of the Body of Christ — equipping both leaders and everyday believers.

View booklets →

Level 3

Foundation Studies

Seven Study Units building a strong biblical understanding of foundational doctrine, each with a guided review where students select correct answers. After all seven units, students receive a Certificate of Completion. Twenty hours or more of study per unit is recommended.

View course →

Level 4

Bible College Module Course

The in-depth module course, online, structured into three Series with multiple-choice questions at the end of every Study Unit. Each completed Series earns a Certificate; all three earn a Certificate in Christian Studies. Forty hours or more per Module Unit is recommended.

Certificates & diploma awards

Certificate of Completion in Christian Foundation Studies
Awarded upon completion of the seven Foundation Studies.
Certificate of Completion in Theological Foundations
Awarded upon completion of Series 1, Series 2, and Series 3 individually.
Advanced Diploma of Theology
Granted upon satisfactory completion of all assessments across Series 1–3. Internal, non-accredited award comparable to AQF Level 6.

How assessments work

The multiple-choice questions assess understanding of the study material. Each question offers up to four answers (a, b, c, or d); read each carefully before selecting on the 1–100 answer sheet or the online assessment. Performance is evaluated by percentage, and certificates reflect the student's average score.
